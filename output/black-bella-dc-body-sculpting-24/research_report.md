## Content Gaps

### 🔴 High Priority
- **Exact physical address and location clarity** — The Groupon deal is listed under 'Washington DC' but the spa's actual address is in Lanham/Glenarden, MD (suburb). Customers may feel misled about distance and commute, especially those without a car.
- **Parking and transit information** — Customers need to know how to get there. The merchant website mentions proximity to the New Carrollton Metro station and Purple Line, but this is absent from the Groupon page.
- **Number of areas/applicators included per session** — Competitors like Epic MedSpa price per treatment area. Customers need to know if the $414 covers one area (e.g., abdomen only) or multiple areas (flanks + abdomen), as this significantly affects perceived value.
- **Staff credentials and training background** — Venus Bliss MAX is a medical-grade device. Customers want assurance that practitioners are certified. The Groupon page does not mention practitioner credentials or certifications, which is especially important given the merchant also runs a body sculpting training academy.
- **Before/after photos specific to Venus Bliss MAX results** — Before/after imagery is among the top conversion drivers for body contouring deals. The merchant website mentions a 'Before & After Pics' page but this content is not surfaced on the Groupon deal page.
- **BMI eligibility and contraindications** — Venus Bliss is FDA-cleared only for individuals with a BMI of 30 or less. The deal mentions a consultation and refund if ineligible, but does not explain WHY someone might be ineligible. This sets clearer expectations upfront and reduces wasted consultations.

### 🟡 Medium Priority
- **Session duration and what to expect during the appointment** — A standard Venus Bliss treatment takes approximately 25 minutes per area. The consultation may add time. Customers need realistic scheduling expectations, especially first-timers.
- **Detailed cancellation and rescheduling policy** — The fine print references a cancellation policy on the merchant's website and notes missed appointments count as used sessions — but the actual 24-hour cancellation window and fee amounts are not stated on the Groupon page, which may frustrate customers.
- **Recommended number of sessions for visible results** — Clinical data shows results may improve for up to 6 months and multiple treatments are often recommended. Customers may not understand that one session may not be sufficient, leading to disappointment and negative reviews.
- **What to wear/bring and pre-treatment prep instructions** — Venus Bliss protocols require clean skin with no lotions, and cannot be performed over tattoos. Customers benefit from knowing what to avoid before their appointment to ensure the session proceeds as planned.

### ⚪ Low Priority
- **Post-treatment care instructions** — Providers typically recommend massage of the treatment area 1–2x/day for 5–10 minutes post-session to maximize results. This aftercare guidance is absent from the deal page.
- **Mask/COVID policy transparency** — The merchant website states masks are required, but this is not communicated on the Groupon page. Customers should be prepared.
