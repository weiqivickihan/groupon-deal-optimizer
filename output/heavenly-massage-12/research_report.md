# Research Report: Heavenly Massage
_Chicago · spa/wellness_

## Competitor Pricing

Chicago 60-min Swedish/deep-tissue massages range from ~$60–$90 at budget independents to $130–$185 at premium day spas. Heavenly Massage's Groupon price of $99.99 for 60 min sits above the Booksy market average of $60–$90 but is below or at par with mid-to-premium Chicago day spas ($130–$185) — and includes aromatherapy and steam shower enhancements that most bare-bones competitors do not bundle.

## Deal Value Assessment

- **Groupon price:** $99.99
- **Direct booking price:** $100.0
- **Competitor avg:** $117.5
- **Good deal?** ❌ No
- **Original price credibility:** inflated

Heavenly Massage's own website lists a 60-min Swedish or deep tissue session at $100.00 — making the Groupon price of $99.99 effectively a zero-discount deal on the base service, with the 'original price' of $135 appearing significantly inflated (~35% above the website's real rack rate). The aromatherapy and steam shower add-ons bundled into the Groupon listing may account for some of the markup, but the gap between Groupon's stated original price and the actual direct-booking menu price is suspicious and a classic Groupon price inflation pattern. The deal offers modest value vs. mid-tier competitors ($117–$130 range for comparable services), but customers can book direct at essentially the same price and potentially have more scheduling flexibility.

## Content Gaps

### 🔴 High Priority
- **No explicit mention of which Chicago location the deal applies to** — Heavenly Massage operates multiple Chicago-area locations (1163 N State St, 1221 N State Pkwy, Morton Grove, Mount Prospect, Schaumburg, Orland Park). At least one reviewer was sent to the wrong location. This is a critical trust and logistics issue for Groupon buyers.
- **No cancellation or rescheduling policy stated** — Groupon buyers frequently cite frustration with booking flexibility. Without a stated cancellation window or policy, customers may not know what happens if they need to reschedule — a top source of negative reviews on deal platforms.
- **No clarification on what 'Steam Shower Aroma Enhancement' and 'Aromatherapy' actually include** — These are key deal differentiators that justify the price premium over competitors. Customers need to know: Is the steam shower a private room? Is aromatherapy a scented oil choice or a diffuser? CBD oil is in the headline but not in any option description — what does CBD oil application mean in practice?
- **Actual hands-on service time not disclosed in deal copy** — Heavenly Massage's own website discloses that a '60-minute session' includes only 50 minutes of hands-on service (10 minutes for consultation and dressing). This is material information many customers do not expect and could feel misled by.
- **No expiration window or redemption restrictions in fine print** — The deal page lists no fine print. Without knowing the promotional value expiration window, blackout dates, or new-customer restrictions, buyers cannot accurately assess the deal's usability — which increases chargebacks and negative reviews.

### 🟡 Medium Priority
- **Tipping policy and payment method for tips not disclosed** — Multiple reviews flag that card tips are not accepted at the front desk. Customers who don't bring cash may be unable to tip therapists, damaging their experience and the therapist relationship. Proactively noting 'cash tip recommended' sets expectations.
- **No therapist credential or licensing information** — With 19,000+ reviews and a high volume of deals sold, credibility signals like 'all therapists are licensed by the Illinois DFPR' would meaningfully boost conversion, especially for first-time buyers evaluating a Groupon deal.
- **No parking or transit information** — The Gold Coast / Near North Side Chicago location is in a dense urban area. Customers need to know about nearby parking garages, street parking rules, or CTA access to plan their visit — especially relevant since multiple reviewers mentioned traveling to the location.
- **No before/after photos or interior spa photos on deal page** — Deal pages with environment photos (treatment rooms, steam room, amenities) significantly increase purchase confidence. The Yelp listing shows 82 photos but none appear to be surfaced on the Groupon page.
- **No mention of couple's private room details (booking process, availability)** — Two of the four deal options are for couples, but there is no information about whether the couple's room must be requested specifically, whether it's guaranteed, or how to request it — a frequent point of confusion in massage Groupon deals.
