## Deal Value Assessment

- **Groupon price:** $50.0
- **Direct booking price:** $N/A
- **Competitor avg:** $233.0
- **Good deal?** ✅ Yes
- **Original price credibility:** inflated

The $50 Groupon price ($100 face-value credit) is dramatically below the Washington DC market average of $200–$338 for a full detail, representing roughly 78% savings vs. the competitor average — which makes it look compelling on the surface. However, the stated 'original price' of $100 for $100 worth of services is a credit-style voucher structure, not a true service price comparison, and the deal page does not specify which services or vehicle sizes $100 actually covers at the merchant's direct rate. Critically, negative Groupon reviews specifically flag potential price manipulation and coupon honoring issues, which raises a yellow flag around whether the $100 credit will be honored without friction or upcharges at redemption.

## Content Gaps

### 🔴 High Priority
- **Exact service inclusions per dollar amount — what does $100 buy at full price? Which package is included?** — The deal is structured as a '$100 credit toward detailing services,' not a named package. Customers cannot tell if $100 covers a full detail, exterior only, or basic wash/wax. This ambiguity is the #1 driver of negative Groupon reviews for this merchant.
- **Vehicle size pricing tiers — is the Groupon price valid for sedans only, or also SUVs, trucks, and vans?** — The merchant's direct website shows different pricing for different vehicle sizes. No vehicle size restriction is stated on the Groupon page. Customers with SUVs or trucks may arrive expecting full coverage but face upcharges.
- **Surcharge policy for excessively dirty vehicles, pet hair, or heavy staining** — The merchant's own website explicitly states additional charges may apply for heavily soiled vehicles. This is entirely absent from the Groupon fine print and is a known source of customer disappointment and disputes.
- **Exact physical address and location details (the listing is marketed as 'Washington DC' but the shop is in Beltsville, MD — ~10 miles outside DC)** — The shop is located at 5006 Howard Ave, Beltsville, MD 20705 — not in Washington DC proper. Customers expecting a DC-area business may be surprised by the distance, especially those without a car or relying on public transit.
- **Mobile vs. in-shop distinction — does the Groupon apply to mobile service or shop-only, and if mobile, what is the additional mobile fee?** — Shine My Ride charges an $80 flat fee for single-car mobile service. If a customer expects mobile service and the Groupon is shop-only, or if they're unaware of the mobile upcharge, this creates confusion and unexpected costs.

### 🟡 Medium Priority
- **Hours of operation** — The shop is closed Monday and Sunday, and closes at 1pm on Saturdays. Many working customers assume weekend availability. No hours are listed on the Groupon deal page.
- **Appointment booking process and lead time** — The merchant requires a $30 deposit and 48-hour cancellation notice for appointments. This policy is not reflected in the Groupon fine print. Customers expecting walk-in service may be turned away.
- **Before/after photos of actual detailing results** — Detailing is a highly visual service. Customers compare providers largely on transformation photos. Competitors like Sharp Detail prominently feature before/after galleries. Absence of photos reduces conversion and perceived credibility.
- **Staff credentials, certifications, or experience callouts** — Shine My Ride was founded in 2006 and has served major corporate clients (Lockheed Martin, CISCO, WTOP). None of this credibility appears on the Groupon page, which is a missed trust-building opportunity.
- **Cancellation and no-show policy detail beyond the generic Groupon boilerplate** — The merchant charges a forfeitable $30 deposit for no-shows or insufficient notice changes. This materially affects consumer risk but is absent from deal fine print.

### ⚪ Low Priority
- **What customers should do to prepare (remove personal items, disclose pet hair or heavy soiling in advance)** — The merchant's website explicitly instructs customers to remove personal items and warns of extra charges for dirty vehicles. Informing buyers pre-purchase sets expectations and reduces disputes.
- **Waiting area amenities (free Wi-Fi, coffee, clean restrooms)** — Details like free Wi-Fi and complimentary coffee are genuine conversion differentiators noted positively in customer reviews, yet are absent from the deal page.
