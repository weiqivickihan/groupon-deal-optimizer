# Optimization Proposal: Refills Health

## Executive Summary
Refills Health's Groupon deal has genuine price leadership — $181/mo tirzepatide undercuts Lemonaid ($299/mo) and Hims ($199/mo) by a material margin — but the deal is structurally broken by three integrity problems: an unverified DC service exclusion that may render the voucher unredeemable for the entire targeted audience, inflated "original" prices that overstate the discount by 30–58 percentage points, and a buried auto-renewal rate that exceeds Refills' own direct pricing. The single highest-leverage fix is resolving and disclosing DC eligibility, which — if the exclusion is real — makes every other optimization irrelevant.

---

## 1. Title Rewrite _(High Impact)_

**Current:** Semaglutide or Tirzepatide + Glycine Weight Loss (Any Dosage): Monthly Program from Refills Health - Up to 64%

**Proposed:** Online GLP-1 Weight Loss Program (Semaglutide or Tirzepatide) — Flat Monthly Price, Any Dosage | Refills Health

**Why:** "Up to 64%" is misleading vs. actual direct-price savings of ~6–30% (deal verdict analysis); removing it reduces chargeback risk. "Flat monthly price, any dosage" directly mirrors the top positive review theme ("no increases for dose changes") and is a concrete differentiator vs. Hims and Lemonaid, which charge more at higher doses.

## 2. Pricing & Value Framing _(High Impact)_

**Problem:** Stated discounts of 55–64% are calculated against inflated list prices ($299/$449) that are ~88% higher than Refills' actual published direct prices ($159/$259), making the discount figure legally and reputationally risky.

**Fix — Add this value callout beneath each price option:**

> **Tirzepatide: $181/mo** — Less than half the $299–$460/mo you'd pay at Lemonaid, Hims, or local DC clinics. Price stays flat even as your dose increases.

> **Semaglutide: $149/mo** — At or below every major telehealth competitor. Includes virtual consult, licensed provider, and shipping.

**Rationale:** Anchoring to real competitor prices (Lemonaid $299, Hims $199, Rivas $460+) is defensible and actually more persuasive than the inflated strikethrough — the genuine market savings are the stronger story.

**Current:** Get fast, 100% online access to a personalized 4‑week GLP‑1 weight loss program with tirzepatide + Glycine or semaglutide, including a virtual medical consult, ongoing licensed provider support, and one flat monthly price with no increases for dose changes.

**Proposed:**

- 💰 **Flat $181/mo tirzepatide or $149/mo semaglutide** — your price never increases when your dose does, unlike most GLP-1 programs

- 🔄 **No long-term commitment:** Cancel at least 7 days before your next shipment date to avoid the next charge

**Why:** Five-star reviews cite speed ("received meds in 5 days," "approvals within 12 hours") and flat pricing as top drivers — these are now leads. Negative review themes (shipping delays, cancellation difficulty, eligibility surprises) are pre-empted here to reduce post-purchase disputes and protect the 3.6 rating from worsening.

## 8. Competitive Positioning _(Medium Impact)_

Refills Health's genuine price advantage over Lemonaid ($299/mo tirzepatide), Hims ($199/mo semaglutide), and DC-area in-person clinics ($460–$660/mo) is the deal's strongest conversion lever — but it is currently buried. Lead with:

> **Why pay $299/mo at Lemonaid or $199/mo at Hims?** Refills Health on Groupon delivers the same GLP-1 medication, prescribed by a licensed U.S. provider, for as low as $149/mo — with a price that never increases as your dose goes up.

Position the "any dosage, flat price" mechanic explicitly against Hims and Lemonaid, which either don't offer tirzepatide or charge higher tiers at higher doses — this is a factual, defensible claim drawn from competitor pricing data and the top positive review theme.

Contact Refills Health's account manager today and request written confirmation of the exact list of U.S. states they are legally authorized to serve — specifically whether Washington DC is included or excluded.
