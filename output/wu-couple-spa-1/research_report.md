## Deal Value Assessment

- **Groupon price:** $149.0
- **Direct booking price:** $N/A
- **Competitor avg:** $109.5
- **Good deal?** ❌ No
- **Original price credibility:** inflated

At $149 (Option 2) and $179 (Option 1), Wu Couple Spa's Groupon prices are significantly above comparable competitors in the Chicago area: Fantastic Feeling Spa offers a near-identical 60-min couples massage with hot stones AND CBD oil for ~$75 on Groupon, and Pure Serenity Spa starts at $89 for couples with hot stones. The stated 'original price' of $269 for the CBD couples massage is highly suspect — it is nearly 3x the going market rate for equivalent services on Groupon, a classic inflated anchor price tactic. The $179 Groupon price for Option 1 is itself above market average, making the 'deal' framing misleading. The direct booking price from the merchant's website (wucouplespa.biz) was not published online, but Yelp/review sources confirm Groupon rates are essentially the merchant's operational pricing. Consumers should be aware that the $269 'original' price is almost certainly not a regularly charged rack rate.

## Content Gaps

### 🔴 High Priority
- **Groupon voucher is valid ONLY on weekdays (Monday–Thursday), not weekends** — This is a critical restriction missing from the deal page fine print shown to buyers. Multiple reviewers were blindsided by this, leading to complaints and unexpected charges. Most couples target weekends for spa days — this will cause significant buyer frustration and redemption failures.
- **Actual hands-on massage time vs. total session time breakdown** — Reviewers report that the 60-minute session includes sauna time and waiting, resulting in only ~30 minutes of actual massage. This is materially different from what '60-minute massage' implies to a buyer and is a significant misrepresentation risk.
- **Upsell and add-on fee transparency** — Multiple reviewers report being charged additional fees ($30+) not disclosed on the Groupon page. Buyers need to know what is and isn't included so they can budget appropriately and won't feel deceived at checkout.
- **CBD oil contraindication disclosure (pregnancy/breastfeeding)** — The merchant's own website states CBD treatment will NOT be performed for pregnant or breastfeeding clients, but this is absent from the Groupon deal page. Affected buyers could purchase a deal they cannot redeem.
- **Exact location and clarification that spa is in Oak Park, IL (not Chicago proper)** — The deal is listed under 'Chicago' but the spa is at 314 Madison St, Oak Park, IL — a suburb ~10 miles west of downtown. Buyers expecting a Chicago location may be surprised. Distance and transit options should be disclosed.

### 🟡 Medium Priority
- **Sauna description accuracy** — The deal references 'sauna' as a feature, but reviewers consistently report it is a small, basic hot room with no steam — not a traditional sauna experience. The description creates unrealistic expectations.
- **Therapist credentials, licensing, and staff bios** — Customers paying $149–$179 for a specialty CBD/hot stone treatment want assurance of licensed massage therapists. No credentials are mentioned anywhere on the deal page.
- **Tipping policy and expectations** — Reviewers specifically flag 'heavy focus on tip' as a negative. Transparency about tipping norms and whether gratuity is included would reduce post-visit friction and negative reviews.
- **Parking and transit instructions** — No information about parking availability near 314 Madison St, Oak Park, or nearby CTA/Metra access. Practical logistics matter for first-time visitors.
- **Before/after service photos of treatment rooms and facilities** — The deal page appears to have limited or no photos of the actual spa interior, sauna, or treatment rooms. Visual trust signals are critical for spa bookings.
- **Cancellation and rescheduling policy** — No cancellation or rescheduling window is stated. Given the appointment-required model and 120-day promotional expiry, buyers need to know what happens if they need to cancel.

### ⚪ Low Priority
- **What to bring / what to wear instructions** — First-time spa visitors, especially couples, often want guidance on what to wear, whether to shower beforehand, and what amenities (robes, towels, lockers) are provided.
- **CBD oil brand or quality details** — Health-conscious buyers want to know the CBD product used — concentration, brand, topical vs. ingested, third-party tested. This builds trust in the premium $179 price point.
