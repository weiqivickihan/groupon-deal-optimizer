- **Original price credibility:** inflated

At $59 for 2 private lessons + 1 group class + 1 practice session, the Groupon price is a genuine bargain versus market rates: individual private lessons in Alexandria run $79–$115/hr at comparable studios, making the implied per-session value compelling. However, the listed 'original price' of $250 is not verifiable from Arthur Murray Alexandria's public pricing (no menu is published), and $250 for this introductory bundle is almost certainly inflated to manufacture the 79% discount optic — a common Groupon pattern. The real discount vs. actual competitor single-lesson rates is still meaningful (~30–40%), but buyers should know the Groupon is explicitly an introductory offer designed to convert them to high-priced packages (which can run into hundreds or thousands of dollars).

## Content Gaps

### 🔴 High Priority
- **No lesson duration specified** — Customers cannot assess value without knowing if private lessons are 30, 45, or 60 minutes each — this is the single most important pricing comparison variable
- **No dance styles listed for the Groupon package** — The studio teaches 29+ styles (ballroom, swing, Latin, country western, etc.) but the deal page does not specify which styles are available in the intro package, leaving customers uncertain about fit
- **Upsell/sales pitch not disclosed** — Multiple reviews confirm attendees are pitched expensive long-term packages at their lesson; transparency would reduce post-visit negative sentiment and buyer surprise, especially for deal-seekers who have no intention of committing to ongoing lessons
- **Exact studio address and parking details** — The Groupon page vaguely states 'patrons can find places to park in the area' but provides no specifics; the studio appears to have relocated (Yelp lists 6489 Little River Turnpike; official site lists 3223 Duke St Suite B1) — address ambiguity will cause confusion
- **Whether the deal is per person or per couple** — A major audience segment is wedding couples; it is entirely unclear if $59 covers one person or both partners — this ambiguity could deter the most motivated buyer segment

### 🟡 Medium Priority
- **Instructor credentials and certification info** — Competitors like Dance Studio Lioudmila and Art of Ballroom actively showcase instructor credentials; Arthur Murray's deal page only says 'trained instructors,' which is vague and does not inspire confidence in comparison
- **Voucher expiration / redemption window** — Fine print does not state how long the voucher is valid; this is a common source of customer frustration and disputes on Groupon deals
- **What to wear / what to bring** — First-time dance students often don't know whether to bring dance shoes, wear specific attire, or arrive early; this practical info reduces friction and no-shows
- **No before/after testimonial photos or video** — The merchant website references a video about dance lessons but nothing appears on the Groupon page; visual social proof is highly persuasive for a performance-based activity
- **Group class schedule / frequency not specified** — The package includes 1 group class but no information on when group classes are held, how large they are, or what style is taught — customers may find scheduling difficult post-purchase

### ⚪ Low Priority
- **Cancellation / rescheduling policy details** — Fine print only says 'merchant's standard cancellation policy applies' with no specifics; customers who need to reschedule will be uncertain about their rights and potential fees
