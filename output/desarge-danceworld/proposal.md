# Optimization Proposal: DeSarge Danceworld

## Executive Summary
DeSarge's 5.0/383-review rating and sub-$27 entry price against a $107+ Chicago competitor average represent a genuinely exceptional deal with an unusually strong trust foundation. The biggest weakness is geographic ambiguity — the studio is in Naperville, 30 miles from Chicago, and nowhere on the deal page is this disclosed — creating a refund-risk purchase for uninformed buyers. The highest-leverage single fix is injecting instructor credentials (Sarge's 40+ years, named instructors) directly into the H1/highlights, because reviews prove the instructor is the primary conversion driver in this category.

---

## 1. Title Rewrite _(High Impact)_
**Current:** Join DeSarge Danceworld for private lessons, group classes, and open dance sessions with up to 72% off

**Proposed:** Learn Ballroom & Latin Dance from a 40-Year Veteran — Private Lessons, Group Classes & Open Dance Sessions in Naperville (Up to 72% Off)

**Why:** Reviews consistently cite Sarge's expertise as the reason for purchase ("I learned more from Sarge in one hour than I learned in a year at other studios"). Adding the instructor credential and the Naperville location simultaneously lifts conversion quality and eliminates post-purchase geographic disappointment — a top content gap flagged as HIGH risk.

## 2. Pricing & Value Framing _(High Impact)_
**Problem:** Discount depth is shown but never anchored against what comparable Chicago studios actually charge, leaving buyers unable to contextualize the savings.

**Fix — add a value-anchor callout beneath each option:**

> **Option 1 — 1 Private Lesson + Open Dance: $26.99**
> *(Comparable private lessons at Duet Dance Studio Chicago: $105–$125/hr. You're paying less than 26 cents on the dollar.)*

> **Option 2 — 3 Private Lessons + 3 Open Dance Sessions: $74.99**
> *(That's $25/lesson. Ballroom Dance Chicago charges up to $235/hr for an experienced instructor.)*

**Rationale:** Competitor anchor pricing (Duet at $105–$125, Ballroom Dance Chicago at $105–$235) is real and documented; framing the Groupon price against named competitors converts price sensitivity into perceived urgency without fabricating scarcity.
