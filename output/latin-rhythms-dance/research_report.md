# Research Report: Latin Rhythms Dance
_Chicago · activities_

## Competitor Pricing

Chicago Latin dance competitors range widely: Dance Academy of Salsa charges $20/class or 6 for $100 (i.e., ~$67 for 4 classes), Dance Center Chicago charges $25/week (~$100/month for 4 classes), and Latin Street Music & Dancing offers structured 4-class and unlimited monthly packages. The Groupon price of $42 for 4 classes is competitive at the low end of the market.

- **Dance Academy of Salsa** — $20/class or 6 for $100 (~$67 for 4 classes) (Salsa, Mambo, Bachata, Cha Cha Cha group classes; 25 years in business; available 7 days/week)
  Source: https://www.danceacademyofsalsa.com/
- **Dance Center Chicago** — $100/month (4 Wednesdays at $25/week) (Salsa and Bachata performance team classes; $25/week for months with 4 classes; intermediate/advanced level)
  Source: https://www.dancecenterchicago.com/performance-class
- **Latin Street Music & Dancing** — Multiple tiers; 4-class packages and unlimited monthly passes available (50+ weekly group classes in Salsa, Bachata, Cumbia and more; structured 4-class and unlimited monthly packages; free parking lot; South Loop location)
  Source: https://www.latinstreetdancing.com/currentclasses/pricing/

## Content Gaps

### 🔴 High Priority
- **Exact class schedule and available time slots** — Customers need to know if classes are available when they can attend before purchasing. The studio operates Mon–Thu 5–10pm and Sat 10am–3pm, which is narrow and not surfaced on the deal page.
- **Direct booking / rack rate price not publicly listed** — Without a transparent direct price, buyers cannot verify the 58% discount is real. The merchant's pricing page only discusses membership structures, not per-class rates.
- **Beginner class availability and waitlist warning** — A Groupon review explicitly warns that beginner classes fill up fast. Customers who purchase may be unable to redeem promptly. This should be prominently disclosed.
- **Redemption process clarity** — The Groupon page itself flags 'a complicated starting process' and requires customers to visit the merchant website post-purchase. Step-by-step instructions would reduce friction and negative reviews.
- **New customers only restriction** — The Groupon listing (via the deal page source) indicates this is 'not valid for clients active within the past 12 months,' but the deal page highlights do not call this out clearly. Existing students may purchase and be denied redemption.

### 🟡 Medium Priority
- **What to wear / footwear requirements** — Competitor Latin Street explicitly states that street shoes, rubber soles, and strapless sandals are not allowed and recommends suede/leather studio shoes. Latin Rhythms' deal page mentions nothing about footwear, which could surprise first-timers.
- **Parking and transit information** — The studio is located at 210 N Racine Ave in the West Loop. No parking guidance is given. Customers in Chicago need to know about street parking, nearby lots, or CTA access before committing.
- **Instructor credentials and bios** — Artistic Director Maricza Valentín has been teaching since 1988 and holds a degree from Columbia College Chicago in dance performance, teaching, and choreography — a major credibility asset that is buried on the Groupon page and not highlighted visually.
- **Whether a dance partner is required** — Solo buyers need explicit reassurance. The Groupon page does note 'no dance partner required' in fine print, but it is not a visible headline callout. This is a common hesitation point for first-time buyers.
- **Class size / student-to-instructor ratio** — Buyers want to know if they'll get individual attention or be lost in a crowd. No class size data is mentioned on the deal page.
- **Promotional value expiration (90 days)** — The promotional value expires 90 days after purchase (per Groupon fine print), but this is not surfaced prominently on the deal page. Combined with classes filling fast, buyers may lose value if not clearly warned.

### ⚪ Low Priority
- **Before/after student progress photos or video** — Social proof of student transformation is a strong conversion driver for fitness and dance deals. The deal page relies on text only.
