# Optimization Proposal: Latin Rhythms Dance

## Executive Summary
Latin Rhythms' 4.8-star rating with 92% five-star reviews and a price point ~50% below Chicago market average (~$10.50/class vs. $17–25 at competitors) is an exceptionally strong conversion foundation. The biggest weakness is pre-purchase anxiety around redemption: a live review explicitly warns of "a complicated starting process" and beginner classes that "fill up fast," yet the deal page offers zero mitigation. The highest-leverage fix is a structured redemption FAQ block with class schedule, new-customer restriction disclosure, and step-by-step booking instructions — this directly addresses the #1 abandonment driver without requiring merchant changes.

---

## 1. Title Rewrite _(High Impact)_
**Current:** One Month of Dance Classes for One Person at Latin Rhythms Dance (58% Off)

**Proposed:** 4 Salsa, Bachata & Mambo Classes at Chicago's Best-Rated Latin Dance Studio — $42 (58% Off)

**Why:** The current title buries the style specificity (Salsa/Bachata/Mambo are search-intent keywords) and omits the social proof anchor ("Best Rated" badge). Surfacing class count ($42 for 4 classes) aligns with how competitors like Dance Academy of Salsa merchandise their offers and reduces post-click surprise.

## 2. Pricing & Value Framing _(High Impact)_
**Problem:** The $90 original price cannot be verified on the merchant site, weakening the 58% anchor credibility.

**Fix — add a value callout block beneath the price:**
> **$10.50 per class — vs. $20–25/class at comparable Chicago Latin dance studios.** Comparable instruction at Dance Academy of Salsa runs ~$17/class (6-class pack); Dance Center Chicago charges $25/class. This is your lowest-cost entry into Chicago's Latin dance scene.

**Rationale:** Competitor benchmarking replaces an unverifiable anchor with an externally validated value claim, which CXL research shows increases purchase intent when original prices are doubted.

**[New Customer Restriction — Prominent Callout]**

**[What to Wear / Footwear Guidance]**
> **What to bring:** Wear comfortable, form-fitting clothes you can move in. **Footwear matters:** Latin dance requires smooth-soled shoes (leather or suede soles recommended). Avoid rubber-soled sneakers, flip-flops, or strapless sandals. Many students purchase a basic pair of ballroom shoes ($20–40) before their first class — ask the studio for recommendations when you book.

*Evidence:* Competitor Latin Street Music & Dancing explicitly prohibits rubber soles and strapless sandals on their class pages. First-timers arriving in wrong footwear cannot fully participate, generating dissatisfaction and poor reviews. No guidance currently exists on the deal page.
