# Research Report: The SPA Club - New York, NY
_New York City · spa/wellness_

## Competitor Pricing

NYC-area Korean jjimjilbang day passes range from ~$50 (Bathhouse Flatiron) to $85 (Spa Castle weekday, Queens). The SPA Club's own direct-booking walk-in rate is $70 weekdays / $90 weekends, making the Groupon 1-person price ($63) meaningfully below its own retail and mid-range vs. competitors.

## Merchant Reputation

- Yelp: 3.7 (477 reviews)
- Google: None (None reviews)

**What customers love:**
- Cleanliness and hygiene: Reviewers on Groupon and social media consistently praise the facility's cleanliness, calling it a 'clean, modern' environment with 'corporate slickness' typical of high-traffic urban Korean spas.
- Attentive and friendly staff: Multiple sources highlight skilled practitioners and welcoming staff as a key reason for repeat visits.
- Body scrub / Korean skincare treatments: The Korean full-body scrub is singled out as a standout service — reviewers describe leaving feeling 'brand new' with 'the smoothest skin.'
- Korean food on-site: The in-house cafeteria serving classic Korean dishes (bulgogi, ramyeon, donkatsu, yuzu tea) is well-received and considered an authentic enhancement to the experience.
- Variety of sauna and bath amenities: The 15,000 sq ft facility with dry saunas, steam rooms, salt room, red clay room, hot tub, and sleeping area earns consistent praise for depth of offering.

**Common complaints:**
- Mixed reviews on Yelp vs. Groupon: The Yelp rating (3.7 / 477 reviews) is notably lower than the Groupon rating (4.5 / 4,024 reviews), suggesting a more divided experience among walk-in / non-deal customers.
- Crowding on weekends: Social media visitors recommend going on weekday evenings (around 7–8pm) to avoid crowds and have saunas largely to oneself.
- Original price credibility concerns: The merchant runs frequent, deep promotional discounts (50% off GA with treatment booking, 50% off for birthday, K-Town restaurant receipt deals, promo codes for 30% off) — suggesting the $90 'list price' may be aspirational rather than what most customers actually pay.
- Smaller scale vs. out-of-city competitors: Compared to Spa Castle (Queens) or SoJo Spa (NJ), The SPA Club is more compact and lacks outdoor facilities or pools, which some visitors flag.

## Deal Value Assessment

- **Groupon price:** $63.0
- **Direct booking price:** $90.0
- **Competitor avg:** $67.5
- **Good deal?** ✅ Yes
- **Original price credibility:** inflated

The Groupon single-admission price of $63 is a genuine improvement over the merchant's own stated walk-in weekend rate of $90 (and $70 weekdays), confirmed by TikTok creators citing those as standard prices. However, the 'original price' credibility is questionable: The SPA Club actively runs 50% off GA promotions tied to treatments, K-Town dining receipts, and birthday discounts, and promo codes (e.g. 'winter30') have brought admission to $49–$63 — meaning savvy direct customers can often match or beat the Groupon price. The two-person Groupon at $119 ($59.50/person) offers slightly better per-head value and is moderately better than Spa Castle's $85 weekday rate for a comparable format. Overall the deal is real but not exceptional — the 'Up to 41% off' headline is based on a weekend walk-in rate that many customers would never pay full price for anyway.

## Content Gaps

### 🔴 High Priority
- **No hours of operation on the deal page** — The spa is open Mon–Sun 8AM–12AM with treatment hours ending at 10PM and dry sauna until 11PM. Customers need this to plan their visit, especially since they cannot redeem within 24 hours of purchase.
- **No exact street address or floor number** — The SPA Club is on the 4th floor of 22 W 32nd St — easy to miss in a dense Koreatown building. First-timers report confusion. The deal page should surface '22 W 32nd St, 4th Floor' prominently.
- **No explanation of what 'complimentary amenities' includes vs. what costs extra** — General admission covers loungewear, locker, towels, toiletries, dry saunas, steam room, bath area, sleeping room, and WiFi — but food, beverages, body scrubs, massages, and facials are all extra. This is not clear on the deal page, and customers who expect a 'full spa day' may feel misled.
- **No mention of the 24-hour advance purchase requirement** — Groupon fine print buries the rule that vouchers must be purchased at least 24 hours before redemption. A tourist buying a same-day pass will be denied entry. This is a high-friction surprise.
- **No 'what to bring' or first-timer etiquette guide** — Korean jjimjilbang etiquette (nudity in gender-separated areas, provided spa uniforms, towel head wrap tradition, etc.) is unfamiliar to many NYC customers. A brief FAQ or 'what to expect' section would reduce anxiety and increase conversion, especially among first-timers.
- **No tattoo policy disclosed** — Many Korean spas have tattoo restrictions. The deal page does not mention The SPA Club's policy, which could result in denied entry and customer disputes.

### 🟡 Medium Priority
- **No parking information** — The spa is in Midtown Manhattan (32nd St / Koreatown). Nearby parking garages exist but are expensive; subway access (34th St-Herald Sq, 33rd St Penn) is the preferred option. Listing transit directions reduces friction for out-of-towners.
- **No photos of the actual facility on the deal page** — The spa has 15,000 sq ft of distinctive spaces (red clay room, Himalayan salt room, hot tubs, sleeping area). Visual proof of quality would significantly improve conversion, especially since the Yelp rating (3.7) is lower than the Groupon rating and some customers have 'heard mixed reviews.'
- **No mention of café/food options** — A full Korean food menu (bulgogi, ramyeon, yuzu tea, donkatsu) is a major differentiator and social media draw for this location. Mentioning the café upsells the experience and sets expectations for a longer, more satisfying visit.
- **No weekend vs. weekday crowd/experience differentiation** — Experienced visitors strongly recommend weekday evenings for a quieter, more intimate experience. Weekends can be crowded. This intel helps customers choose when to redeem and improves satisfaction.

### ⚪ Low Priority
- **Groupon rating source not contextualized vs. Yelp** — The 4.5 Groupon rating (4,024 reviews) vs. 3.7 Yelp rating (477 reviews) is a meaningful discrepancy. Groupon reviews are exclusively from deal purchasers, which may skew positive due to anchoring on value. Savvy customers who check Yelp before buying may hesitate.
- **No age minimum or children policy** — Some Korean spas restrict minors, especially in nudity-required bathing areas. Families purchasing the 2-person deal need to know if children are permitted.
